Priority and Assiciativity of C's operators

  • Table of priority and associativity of C's operators

    • Priority and Associativity of the operators of C:

        Operators Associativity
          ( )   [ ]   . (member)   -> (pointer member)   ++ (post)   -- (post)   left to right
          (Unary) +   (Unary) -   !   ~   ++ (pre)   -- (pre)   & (referenec)   * (de-reference)   (typecast)   sizeof(..)     right to left
          *   /   %     left to right
          +   -     left to right
          >>   <<     left to right
          <   <=   >   >=     left to right
          ==   !=     left to right
          &     left to right
          ^     left to right
          |     left to right
          &&     left to right
          ||     left to right
          ?:     right to left
          =   +=   -=   *=   /=   %=   >>=   <<=   &=   ^=   |=     right to left
          ,     left to right

  • Advice

    • I don't memorize the exact order

    • I do memorize the most commonly used combinations:

        • Relative priorities of arithmetic operators:

            • Unary operation always first:   +   -       
            •   *   /   %     
            •   +   -

        • Relative priorities of compare and logic operators:

            • Unary operation always first: !
            • Compare operations first: <   <=   >   >=     
            •                                              ==   !=

            • And is like multiply:          &&
            • Or is like add:                   ||

        • The assignment operators have the lowest priotity (I don't use the comma operator much - except in the for-statement to initailize multiple variables)

            • I use brackets in cases where I am not certain which operator has a higher priority...
